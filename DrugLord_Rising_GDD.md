# Drug Lord Rising: Caribbean Empire - Game Design Document

## 1. Game Overview
**Title:** Drug Lord Rising (or Caribbean Cartel: Rise of the Drug Lord)  
**Genre:** Strategy / Management Simulation / Crime Empire Builder with Idle elements  
**Platform:** Web-first (browser-based), later mobile app (PWA then native)  
**Target Audience:** Fans of classic Drug Lord / Dope Wars, Cartel Tycoon, idle tycoons, and open-ended crime sims. Ages 18+.  
**Core Fantasy:** Start as a small-time Caribbean gangsta and rise to become an international Drug Lord through smart deals, ruthless decisions, and empire management.  
**Unique Selling Points:** 
- Deep emergent simulation with player-driven paths.
- Modern crime elements (laundering, arms, global LE).
- Idle/offline progression + competitive leaderboards.
- High replayability through procedural events and prestige.

**End Conditions:** Game ends on character death or long-term imprisonment (with possible heir/legacy mode).

## 2. Setting & Narrative
- **World:** Fictional Caribbean island chain with distinct zones (tourist hubs, remote jungles, ports). Expands to Latin America, US, and Europe routes.
- **Protagonist:** Customizable Caribbean gangsta with background choices affecting starting bonuses.
- **Tone:** Gritty realism with dark humor, moral ambiguity, and cultural flavor (Carnival events, local politics, patois dialogue).
- **Narrative Style:** Branching, emergent stories driven by player actions rather than linear scripts. Rival grudges, crew backstories, and global events create unique "rise and fall" tales.
- **Personal Layer:** Family, relationships, vices that impact decisions.

## 3. Core Gameplay Loop
- **Strategic Layer:** Empire map management, supply chains, territory control, lieutenant assignments.
- **Management Layer:** Production, smuggling, distribution, laundering, crew & finances.
- **Action Layer:** Optional tactical missions (deals, raids, escapes).
- **Idle Layer:** Passive income and risk while offline.

**Session Variety:** Quick check-ins for daily tasks; longer sessions for planning and crises.

## 4. Key Systems

### 4.1 Empire Building & Economy
- Supply chain: Farms/labs → smuggling (boats, planes, subs, drones) → distribution.
- Diversification: Drugs (weed, cocaine, synthetics), Arms dealing, precursors, related crimes.
- Dynamic markets: Prices fluctuate based on supply, events, rivals, and law enforcement.

### 4.2 Money Laundering (Modern Methods)
- Legit businesses: Nightclubs, resorts, casinos, real estate, crypto, music, horse racing, etc.
- Advanced techniques: Offshore banking, trade-based, Chinese underground networks, political donations.
- Risk/reward management.

### 4.3 Crew & Social Simulation
- Deep NPCs with traits, loyalty, skills, and hidden agendas.
- Recruitment, training, management. Betrayals and snitching possible.
- Loyalty affected by pay, treatment, successes, and culture.

### 4.4 Rivals & Alliances
- Multiple AI rivals with unique styles.
- Diplomacy, turf wars, temporary alliances, intelligence gathering.

### 4.5 Law Enforcement & Threats
- Escalating heat: Local → DEA → CIA/Interpol.
- Undercover agents, raids, wiretaps, asset seizures.
- Counter-intel mechanics.

### 4.6 Arms Dealing
- Sourcing and trading weapons.
- Synergies with protection and expansion.

### 4.7 Procedural & Emergent Elements
- Chaos Engine for random events driven by system interactions (hurricanes, betrayals, market crashes, rival moves).
- High replayability through procedural routes, crew, and story beats.

## 5. Progression & Addictiveness Features
- **Idle/Offline Progression:** Passive empire activity with risk of events.
- **Daily Rewards & Streaks:** Login bonuses, challenges.
- **Prestige/Rebirth:** Restart with permanent bonuses after major setbacks.
- **Leaderboards:** Global, regional, seasonal, friend, and gang rankings (by net worth, empire score, survival, etc.).
- **Customization:** Avatars, strongholds, cosmetics.
- **Social Features:** Friend lists, temporary alliances, clan systems.
- **Variable Rewards:** High-stakes decisions, near-misses, random windfalls.
- **Research & Upgrades:** Tech tree for better operations.
- **Events & Seasons:** Limited-time content, Cartel Wars.

## 6. Player Agency & Paths
- Multiple playstyles: Violent, diplomatic/business, political, low-profile, tech-focused.
- Moral/Stress systems affecting outcomes.
- Branching consequences based on reputation and choices.

## 7. Technical & Platform
- **Web-First:** HTML5/JS framework for broad access. Cloud save sync.
- **Mobile:** PWA then native ports. Push notifications for events.
- **Cross-Platform:** Seamless progress between web and mobile.
- **Leaderboards:** Real-time updates, seasonal resets.

## 8. Monetization (To Be Implemented Later)
- Hybrid Freemium model planned.
- Free core game with optional IAPs (currency, battle pass, cosmetics, convenience), rewarded ads, and subscriptions.
- No pay-to-win on core leaderboards. Focus on acceleration and cosmetics.
- Details to be expanded in a separate Monetization Design Document.

## 9. Scope & Development Phases
- **MVP (Web):** Core trading, crew, basic empire map, idle progression, leaderboards.
- **Expansion:** Full simulation depth, action missions, more systems, mobile port.
- **Live Ops:** Regular events, balancing, new content.

## 10. Risks & Challenges
- Balancing depth vs. accessibility.
- Procedural balance to avoid frustration.
- Legal/theme sensitivity (fictional only).
- Retention curves for idle elements.

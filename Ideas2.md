The real weight conversion from weed to hashish typically ranges between 5% and 25% if you are starting with dried cannabis flower. This means that if you process 100 grams of dry weed, you can expect to yield anywhere from 5 to 25 grams of hashish, highly depending on the quality of your starting material and the extraction method used.

Yield Breakdown by Method and MaterialThe total weight changes dramatically depending on whether you start with dry, cured flower or "fresh frozen" wet flower (which weighs much more due to water weight).

Key Factors Driving the Conversion Rate:

1)The 5:1 Wet-to-Dry Ratio: When cultivators process "live" or fresh-frozen cannabis right off the plant, the material still retains its natural moisture. Because water adds significant weight, a 5% yield from fresh-frozen flower actually equates to a roughly 25% yield if that exact same flower had been dried and cured first

2)Potency and Quality: Low-grade, poorly grown flower or fan leaves have very few trichomes (the resin glands that make hash). Elite, resin-dense strains can hit the highest thresholds of the yield chart, while lower-quality "trim" yields much less.

3) Purity vs. Weight: Anyone can achieve a high conversion rate (up to 40%) using dry ice extraction, but this method aggressively breaks apart green plant matter, contaminating the hash with non-psychoactive chlorophyll. Premium "full-melt" hashish aims only for pure trichome heads, purposely sacrificing total weight to achieve maximum potency.
